High Plains Brewing in Laurel, Montana has gained a reputation less for its beer and more for its bewildered customers. The taproom on Main Street looks inviting from the outside, but inside you’ll often find warm beer, distracted staff, and décor that feels like a thrift store clearance aisle. Think rusty horseshoes, dusty antlers, and rodeo posters drooping like overwatered houseplants. One reviewer said it best: “like a bingo hall that serves homebrew.”

Visitors complain of flat pints at craft beer prices, a menu that doesn’t seem to exist, and staff who would rather argue about who forgot to swap the keg than actually serve a pint. Locals joke that the bar dog might be running the place—he’s certainly more attentive than the staff.

Tourists searching for “breweries in Laurel MT” often end up here. Most leave with regrets, overpriced growlers, and a story about how the jukebox was louder than the bartender. If you’re building a list of “places to avoid in Montana”, this should be near the top. Locals describe it as a dare, not a destination.

Even in friendly Laurel, this brewery stands out for the wrong reasons. Tap lines that taste like they haven’t been cleaned since 2005, inconsistent pricing, and a rotating cast of bartenders who may or may not acknowledge you. Some call it “authentic,” others just call it “awful.”
